Weyl's Lemma. Let $\Omega \subseteq \mathbb{R}^N$ be open. If $u \in \mathfrak{D}^{\prime}(\Omega ; \mathbb{R})$ (the space of Schwartz distributions on $\Omega$) satisfies $\Delta u=f \in C^{\infty}(\Omega ; \mathbb{R})$ in the sense that \[ \langle\Delta \psi, u\rangle=\langle\psi, f\rangle, \quad \psi \in C_{\mathrm{c}}^{\infty}(\Omega ; \mathbb{R}), \] then $u \in C^{\infty}(\Omega ; \mathbb{R})$. Â  Proof. Set \[ \gamma_t(x)=(4 \pi t)^{-\frac{N}{2}} \exp \left[-\frac{|x|^2}{4 t}\right] . \] Given $x_0 \in \Omega$, choose $r>0$ so that $\bar{B}\left(x_0, 3 r\right) \subset \subset \Omega$ and $\eta \in C_{\mathrm{c}}^{\infty}\left(B\left(x_0, 3 r\right) ;[0,1]\right)$ so that $\eta=1$ on $\bar{B}\left(x_0, 2 r\right)$. Set $v=\eta u$ and $w=\Delta v-\eta f$. Then $w$ is supported in $B\left(x_0, 3 r\right) \backslash \bar{B}\left(x_0, 2 r\right)$. Now take \[ v_t(x)=\gamma_t \star v(x) \equiv\left\langle\gamma_t(\cdot-x), v\right\rangle \\ w_t(x)=\gamma_t \star w(x)\equiv\left\langle\gamma_t(\cdot-x), w\right\rangle . \] For each $t>0, v_t$ is smooth. Moreover, \[ \dot{v}_t(x)=\left\langle\gamma_t(\cdot-x), \eta f\right\rangle+\left\langle\gamma_t(\cdot-x), w\right\rangle \] and so \[ v_t(x)=v_1(x)-\int_t^1 \gamma_\tau \star(\eta f)(x) d \tau-\int_t^1 w_\tau(x) d \tau . \]
